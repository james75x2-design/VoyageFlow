# Tokyo in Spring: VoyageFlow Destination Guide

## Overview

Spring in Tokyo runs from March through May and is one of the most popular times to visit. The season is defined by cherry blossom (sakura) viewing, mild temperatures (10–20°C / 50–68°F), and a packed cultural calendar. VoyageFlow recommends booking accommodations 3–6 months in advance for late March and early April travel.

## Cherry Blossom Season

Peak sakura typically falls between late March and early April, though exact dates shift year to year based on weather. VoyageFlow surfaces top hanami (flower viewing) spots including Ueno Park, Shinjuku Gyoen, Chidorigafuchi, and the Meguro River. For the most current bloom forecast, VoyageFlow suggests users check the Japan Meteorological Corporation's annual sakura report.

## Suggested Spring Itinerary

A typical VoyageFlow-generated 5-day Tokyo spring itinerary covers:

- **Day 1:** Arrival, Shinjuku exploration, evening at Omoide Yokocho
- **Day 2:** Asakusa, Senso-ji Temple, Sumida River sakura cruise
- **Day 3:** Harajuku, Meiji Shrine, Yoyogi Park hanami picnic
- **Day 4:** Day trip to Kamakura or Nikko for temple visits and gardens
- **Day 5:** Ueno Park, museum district, departure

VoyageFlow adjusts itineraries based on trip length, traveler pace, and interests such as food, culture, shopping, or nightlife.

## Booking Considerations

Spring is peak season, and VoyageFlow flags several booking constraints:

- Hotel rates in central Tokyo (Shinjuku, Shibuya, Ginza) can be 30–50% higher during sakura week
- Flights from North America and Europe often sell out 2–3 months ahead for late-March travel
- Popular ryokan (traditional inns) require booking 4–6 months in advance
- Restaurant reservations at high-demand venues should be secured before arrival
